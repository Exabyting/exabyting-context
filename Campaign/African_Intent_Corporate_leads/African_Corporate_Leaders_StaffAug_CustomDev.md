## Campaign Notes

- **Brevity by design:** Each email targets 50–75 words in the body. Shorter cold emails consistently achieve higher response rates. Every sentence must earn its place.
- **Progressive value per email:** Each follow-up introduces a distinct angle — talent speed (E1), cost of inaction (E2), custom software for African markets (E3), build vs. buy credibility (E4), respectful close (E5). No repetition.
- **Low-friction CTAs:** Use binary questions ("Worth a chat?"), specific time offers ("Tuesday or Thursday?"), or one-click booking links. Avoid open-ended requests.
- **Tone calibration for African market:** Professional, warm, and relationship-oriented. African business culture values trust, personal connection, and respect. Lead with partnership and mutual value — never pushy.
- **Regional nuance:** Africa is diverse — tailor messaging by sub-region where possible. West Africa (Nigeria, Ghana) responds to entrepreneurial and growth language. East Africa (Kenya, Rwanda) aligns with innovation and digital transformation. Southern Africa (South Africa, Botswana) may prefer a more formal, corporate tone.
- **Data protection compliance:** Be mindful of South Africa's POPIA, Nigeria's NDPR, and Kenya's Data Protection Act. Ensure lawful basis for outreach. Include an unsubscribe mechanism in your sending platform.
- **Timezone awareness:** Schedule sends between 08:00–10:00 local time — WAT (West Africa), EAT (East Africa), SAST (Southern Africa), CAT (Central Africa).
- **Infrastructure context:** Many African markets are mobile-first with unique infrastructure realities. Position custom development as a strength for building solutions that work within these contexts.
- **Follow-up tracking:** Monitor open and reply rates per email. If Email 3 (custom software angle) outperforms Email 2 (staff augmentation), consider leading with custom dev in future campaigns.
